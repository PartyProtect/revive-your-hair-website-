Affiliate Disclosure

Last Updated: October 15, 2025

⚠️ Transparency Notice: Revive Your Hair participates in affiliate marketing programs. Some links on this website are affiliate links, meaning we may earn a commission if you make a purchase through these links at no additional cost to you.

1. What Are Affiliate Links?

Affiliate links are special tracking URLs that allow companies to identify when a sale came from our website. When you click an affiliate link and make a purchase:

  • The merchant (seller) tracks that you came from our website
  • If you complete a purchase, we receive a small commission
  • You pay the same price — affiliate commissions do not increase your cost
  • The merchant pays us, not you

2. Why We Use Affiliate Links

Affiliate commissions help us:

  • Cover website hosting and maintenance costs
  • Fund research and content creation time
  • Keep the website free for visitors
  • Continue providing evidence-based hair loss information

Without affiliate income, we would need to charge for access to content or rely on intrusive advertising.

3. Our Affiliate Relationships

We may participate in affiliate programs with:

  • Telemedicine services: Hims, Keeps, Ro, Manual, and similar platforms
  • Online pharmacies: Vendors selling finasteride, minoxidil, and related products
  • E-commerce platforms: Amazon Associates and similar programs
  • Equipment vendors: Dermarollers, dermapens, and microneedling devices
  • Supplement companies: Hair loss supplements and vitamins (if recommended)

4. Our Commitment to Transparency

4.1 Clear Disclosure

We clearly disclose affiliate relationships:

  • This dedicated Affiliate Disclosure page
  • Disclosure notices on pages containing affiliate links
  • Clear labeling of affiliate links where appropriate

4.2 Honest Recommendations

We only recommend products and services we genuinely believe in:

  • Recommendations are based on clinical evidence, not commission rates
  • We research products thoroughly before recommending them
  • We would recommend these products even without affiliate commissions
  • We do not promote products solely because they offer high commissions

4.3 Editorial Independence

Our content decisions are independent:

  • Affiliate relationships do not influence our editorial content
  • We maintain objectivity in product reviews and comparisons
  • We discuss both benefits and drawbacks of products
  • We highlight cheaper alternatives when they exist

5. Products We Recommend

5.1 Evidence-Based Criteria

We only recommend products that meet strict criteria:

  • Clinical evidence: Backed by peer-reviewed research
  • Safety: FDA-approved or well-established safety profile
  • Effectiveness: Demonstrated results in clinical trials
  • Quality: Reputable manufacturers and suppliers
  • Value: Reasonable pricing relative to alternatives

5.2 What We Don't Recommend

We will NOT recommend products that:

  • Lack clinical evidence
  • Make unrealistic or false claims
  • Are overpriced compared to equivalent alternatives
  • Have poor safety profiles or serious side effects not disclosed
  • Come from disreputable or unlicensed sellers

6. No Additional Cost to You

Important: Using our affiliate links costs you nothing extra:

  • Prices are the same whether you use our link or go directly to the merchant
  • Some merchants offer exclusive discounts through affiliate links (you may actually save money)
  • You are never charged more because you used our link

7. You Are Never Obligated

Using affiliate links is optional:

  • You can search for products directly instead of using our links
  • You can shop elsewhere if you find better prices
  • We provide product names so you can compare prices
  • We often mention multiple purchasing options, including non-affiliate sources

8. Types of Affiliate Compensation

8.1 Commission-Based

Most common: We earn a percentage of the sale when you purchase through our link.

Example: If you buy a $30 dermaroller through our Amazon affiliate link, we might earn $1-2 (3-7% commission).

8.2 Pay-Per-Lead

Some programs pay for qualified leads (e.g., completing a telemedicine consultation), even if you don't make a purchase.

8.3 Flat-Fee Referrals

Some merchants pay a fixed amount per referral regardless of purchase amount.

9. No Influence on Medical Advice

Critical Clarification:

  • Affiliate relationships do NOT influence our medical information or treatment recommendations
  • We recommend treatments based on clinical evidence, not commission potential
  • Our blog content is evidence-based and would be the same without affiliate links
  • We maintain the same standards whether or not affiliate programs exist

See our Medical Disclaimer for important information about medical content.
