About the Author

What started as personal research turned into this site. My background isn't in medicine—it's in rigorous pursuit of truth. I approach health content the way an investigative journalist approaches a story: follow the evidence, cite your sources, and be honest about what we know and don't know.

My Credentials

I don't have medical credentials, and I won't pretend otherwise. What I do have is the ability to read clinical research, understand statistical methodology, and translate complex medical literature into plain English. The Problem We Saw

Spend 20 minutes googling hair loss treatments and you'll find:

Reddit threads with conflicting anecdotes
"Finasteride ruined my life" next to "Finasteride saved my life" with zero context about the actual data.
YouTube videos selling miracle solutions
Supplements, laser caps, essential oils—all promising results with zero clinical backing.
Medical sites that sound like textbooks
"Androgenetic alopecia presents with progressive miniaturization..." Cool, but what do I actually do?

The information exists. There are decades of clinical studies on what works for hair loss. But it's buried in medical journals behind paywalls, written in technical language, and scattered across hundreds of papers.

So people either pay thousands for treatments they don't understand, or they do nothing because they can't figure out what's real.

What I Actually Do

I read the studies and convert them into something you can understand without a PhD. And I've read a ton of studies because hair loss is a topic that deserves to be thoroughly researched.

You can read my process below.

How I Work

I start with peer-reviewed research
PubMed, medical journals, clinical trials. If it's not published and peer-reviewed, I don't cite it.
I look at sample sizes and methodology
A study with 12 people isn't the same as one with 1,200. I note the difference.
I translate medical language into plain English
"Androgenetic alopecia" becomes "pattern hair loss." "Follicular miniaturization" becomes "your hair is shrinking."
I explain the mechanisms, not just the treatments
Understanding why something works helps you make better decisions about your situation.
I'm honest about limitations
Side effects happen. Not everything works for everyone. Some questions don't have good answers yet. I tell you.

What I'm Not

I'm not a doctor. If you want medical advice, talk to a dermatologist. What I provide is research synthesis—taking what the clinical literature says and making it accessible.

I'm not here to sell you supplements or miracle formulas. The treatments that work have decades of research behind them. They're also mostly generic and available anywhere.

My Standard

If I wouldn't recommend it to someone I care about, I don't put it on this site. That's it. That's the filter.
